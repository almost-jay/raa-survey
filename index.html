About this study

This survey is exploring a specific type of social uncertainty — the experience of feeling unable to locate an adequate point of comparison for your own situation. This is independent research, not affiliated with any institution or funded body.

What this involves:

  • Some questions may prompt reflection on topics you haven't considered before; try to give them genuine consideration and answer honestly.

What this study is about

This survey is exploring whether certain life circumstances — such as occupying an unusual or hard-to-categorise social position — are associated with a specific form of social uncertainty: difficulty locating an adequate point of comparison for one's own situation.

This survey is an exploratory/preliminary investigation into whether certain life circumstances can produce a theoretical form of anomie involving self-doubt. It doesn't mean anything clinically, and is just looking at statistical correlations.
